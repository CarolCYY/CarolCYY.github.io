CLASS 60-cm Half-Wave Plate

Context: This project involved the design and characterization of a critical optical component for the Cosmology Large Angular Scale Surveyor (CLASS) telescope operating at 90 GHz. The half-wave plate is used to modulate the polarization of incoming light, which is essential for isolating the faint cosmic microwave background signal from galactic foregrounds.

My Role & Contributions: I contributed significantly to the mechanical design, modeling, and laboratory characterization phases of this project.

Key Responsibilities & Tasks:

  • Designed and modeled the reflective half-wave plate structure and mounting using SolidWorks.
  • Performed ray-tracing simulations to optimize the geometry for polarization modulation efficiency.
  • Collaborated closely with fabrication specialists during the manufacturing process.
  • Assisted with laboratory characterization measurements to verify performance metrics, comparing experimental results against simulations.
